Block Engine Integration

Jito, Helius, bloXroute, Astralane, ZeroSlot, Nozomi — 7 concurrent submitters engineered for maximum transaction landing rate.

Why Landing Rate Is Everything

In MEV, detecting an opportunity is only half the battle. If your transaction doesn't land in the target slot, you lose. On Solana's high-throughput chain, thousands of transactions compete for inclusion every 400ms. The difference between a 30% and 60% landing rate is the difference between a profitable operation and a losing one.

Our approach: submit through every available channel simultaneously. Each submitter has different network paths, validator relationships, and inclusion characteristics. Multicasting maximizes the probability that at least one path lands your transaction.

Integration Architecture

All 7 submitters operate behind a unified abstraction layer in our Rust codebase. When the execution engine decides to submit a transaction, it:

  1. Prepares variants — For Jito, wraps the transaction in a bundle with tip. For QUIC, serializes with appropriate priority fee. For others, formats per their API requirements.
  2. Fires simultaneously — All 7 submissions launch concurrently via Tokio tasks. No waiting for responses before sending the next.
  3. Tracks results — Each submitter reports back success/failure/timeout independently. The first confirmed landing wins; others are logged for analytics.
  4. Adapts dynamically — If a submitter consistently fails or shows high latency, its weight in the priority ordering is automatically adjusted.

Jito Bundle Deep Dive

Jito bundles deserve special attention because they provide atomic execution ordering — a guarantee that your sequence of transactions executes in exact order without any other transaction interleaved. This is critical for:

  • Sandwich protection: Your swap executes at the price you simulated
  • Multi-step strategies: Flash loan → swap → swap → repay executes atomically
  • Backrunning: Your transaction lands immediately after the target transaction

Our Jito integration includes dynamic tip calculation based on recent landing rates, bundle simulation before submission, and automatic retry with adjusted tips on failure.

Helius Staked Connections

Helius provides staked validator connections that offer priority processing. Unlike public RPC nodes, staked connections have higher rate limits, lower latency, and preferential treatment in validator queues. We maintain persistent connections to minimize handshake overhead.

Monitoring & Analytics

Every submission is tracked with microsecond-precision timestamps:

  • Per-submitter landing rate, latency distribution, and failure modes
  • Cost analysis: Jito tips vs priority fees vs bloXroute fees per successful landing
  • Slot-level analysis: which submitter lands first by slot leader
  • Historical trends: how landing characteristics change with network congestion

All metrics flow to InfluxDB and are visualized in Grafana dashboards, giving you real-time insight into your execution infrastructure's performance.
