Atomic Arbitrage Bot

Cross-DEX price discrepancy exploitation with zero-risk atomic execution. Graph-based path discovery across every major Solana DEX.

How Cross-DEX Arbitrage Works on Solana

Price discrepancies between DEXes are constant and fleeting. When SOL/USDC trades at $148.50 on Raydium and $148.72 on Orca Whirlpools, the $0.22 spread exists for milliseconds. Our bot detects these gaps, constructs an atomic transaction that buys low and sells high, and lands it before the opportunity closes.

The key word is atomic: the entire multi-swap sequence executes in a single Solana transaction. If any step fails — price moved, insufficient liquidity, simulation mismatch — the entire transaction reverts. You never lose capital on a failed arb.

Graph-Based Path Discovery

Our routing engine models the entire Solana DEX landscape as a weighted directed graph. Each pool is an edge, each token is a node. The engine continuously updates edge weights as prices change and runs modified Bellman-Ford to detect negative cycles — which represent profitable arbitrage paths.

This approach discovers complex multi-hop opportunities that simple pair-scanning misses:

  • 2-hop: SOL → USDC (Raydium) → SOL (Orca) — classic triangular arb
  • 3-hop: SOL → BONK → USDC → SOL — cross-token triangular
  • Multi-hop: Paths involving 4+ swaps through lesser-monitored pairs where competition is lower

Flash Loan Integration

For capital-efficient execution, our bots integrate with Marginfi flash loans. Instead of deploying your own SOL or USDC as working capital, the bot borrows within the same transaction, executes the arbitrage, repays the loan plus fee, and keeps the profit.

This means:

  • Zero capital at risk (beyond transaction fees and Jito tips)
  • Scale limited only by flash loan pool depth, not your balance
  • No inventory management or rebalancing needed

Execution Pipeline

  1. Account Monitoring — Geyser gRPC streams all pool account updates. Price changes are decoded in-memory with zero-copy deserialization.
  2. Opportunity Detection — Updated prices feed into the graph engine. Profitable cycles trigger immediately.
  3. Transaction Construction — The optimal swap sequence is compiled into a single transaction with CU budget optimization and priority fee calculation.
  4. Simulation — Fast simulation via Helius or local validator to verify profitability after fees and slippage.
  5. Multicast Submission — Transaction fires through all 7 submitters: Jito bundles, Helius, bloXroute, Astralane, ZeroSlot, Nozomi, direct QUIC.
  6. Result Tracking — Landed transactions are confirmed and PnL is recorded to InfluxDB within seconds.
